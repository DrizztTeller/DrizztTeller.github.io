Analyze your event

  • Explore data from past editions to assess actual usage of resources deployed :
  • Among all the food prepared, how much was actually consumed ?
  • How many workforce members were effectively engaged during peak times
  • How often were the buses operating at full capacity, and how many trips were made with only partial occupancy ?
  • Did the layout and size of the spaces align with attendee behavior and movement patterns ?
  • How many seats designated for guests were actually occupied ?
  • Test alternative planning scenarios, evaluating their impact on costs and experience using past event data:
  • By reducing bus frequency from 15 minutes to 10 minutes : how many buses can be saved and how many people can be carried while ensuring a 20min max waiting time ?
  • By limiting the number of guest for the finals, what would be the projected cost savings across all services, including transportation, catering, energy consumption, and logistics?

Streamline your processes

Provide tools and process to maximize inter departments collaboration and ensure efficient inter dependencies between planning processes.
The Venue Population Model process is one of the key planning process for every event organization. It helps service provider teams nail the numbers and define the resources : buses, meals, security staff, tech gear, and more, by answering the big three :

KEY PROCESS : DEFINE WHO, WHEN, WHERE
Predicting the movements of groups like journalists, photographers, and guests can be particularly challenging but that’s where client owners role is crucial. Their insights are the secret sauce, and when shared seamlessly, they unlock next-level operational efficiency. Re.Events toolbox contains a dedicated tool to smoothly share, update, control and visualize those numbers.

Monitor your operations

  • During the event, effective monitoring of on-field activities and operations is essential, not just to keep things running smoothly, but to swiftly spot and tackle issues as they emerge. Drawing from the Paris 2024 experience, Re.Events integrates various tools and data sources to deliver real-time information. This data is transformed into impactful visualizations that empower operational teams and executives to support efficient decision making.
    Some examples of key operations to monitor during the event :
  • Venue Bump-In progress
  • Event Incidents
  • Arrival and Departure flow
  • Venue Access Control (spectators and accredited)
  • Workforce Noshow (volunteers and contractors)
  • Accreditation delivery
  • On Venue Medical care
  • Competition schedule follow up
  • Transport monitoring

Collect your data

In most events, crucial data on actual resource deployment and utilization is generated in real-time. However, this data often remains with service providers, and, once the event concludes, is either inaccessible or permanently lost. After the event, obtaining high-quality, standardized data becomes a significant challenge : especially when multiple providers are involved, such as food concessionaires across different venues.


To unlock the full potential of event data, it is essential to first identify the data to be collected, then establish a unified format across all providers and all editions, ensuring seamless collection and analysis post-event.


Re.Events offers specialized legal expertise and a robust data engineering framework designed to capture and retain the most valuable data.

Illustrating Our Expertise: Examples of Re.Events Solutions

Dashboard Venue Operation

Thanks to International Olympic Committee and data collected from Tokyo 2020+1, we collaboratively analyzed egress of main venues used during the Games and created a tool to empower business departments extracting participants’ behaviours and insights to right size their operations. Answering very specific questions like “How many wheelchair users were on site at any one time?” but also more global behaviour like “How long before the competitions do journalists arrive on-site?”, never before had an Organising Committee been able to obtain this type of information and this level of precision.

Single Source of Truth

Organizing an event with more than 50 sports, 40 venues, 25 days of competitions, 14,000 athletes competing and 15 million of visitors requires effective coordination of the different operational divisions (transport, security, food and beverage, catering, logistic, …) by sharing key information and ensuring a unique reference to keep every division at the same level of information. From key numbers to very specific information we gathered, displayed and defined the right governance to ensure easy access and up to date numbers.

RealTime data collection and monitoring Dashboards

During the summer of the Games more than 20 dashboards developed for the Main Operations Center, the Control Tower, ensuring smooth running of the Games, supporting decision making and feeding executives daily report. Ranging from real-time monitoring of spectator attendance and workforce no-shows to incident analysis, it gave a visibility rarely seen in the organization of the Games, appreciated by executives and recognized by IOC. The main fan zone of the Games was equipped with best in class and innovative counting solutions allowing real-time visibility on occupancy. Venue team and central team used this real-time data to proactively communicate when venue was nearly crowded to avoid reaching maximum occupancy and creating spectators’ frustration.
